# Memory Drift as Surprisal-Gated Quantisation — and its Convergence with GPP Laughter

### A grounding artifact: the conceptual record of a single conversation that became a natural experiment in human memory, and the way it met the Laughter pillar coming the other direction

---

## What this document is

This is the conceptual record of a conversation — not a specification and not a
finished article, but the irreplaceable middle thing the other two cannot carry:
*why the thinking took the shape it did.* It exists so that a future writer can
introduce the idea to a general readership, and a future researcher can develop

The conversation began as a trivial disagreement about a twenty-year-old manga
memory and ended as a two-directional bridge: **human memory drift** analysed

## The river returned a needle: a memory as apparatus

The Laughter module began when a child-development paper on laughter was read
with a framework in mind and recognised as the substrate of a missing pillar.
This conversation began the same way, with a humbler needle: **a person's own
decades-old memory, offered as fact, that turned out to be a natural experiment
in how memory decays.**

The claim, as first stated: in Naruto, a teacher slams a door at the ninja
academy, and that door-slam is the trigger that turns Sasuke evil — a beat the
fandom supposedly called "doorslam-no-jutsu," "the most powerful jutsu, lost to
time." The initial machine response was to dismiss it as a meme, *without
searching* — the first and most instructive error in the record, because it was
the machine committing exactly the sin the conversation would later anatomise:
treating a confident prior as a complete picture.

What made the memory usable as apparatus is that its owner was **well-calibrated
about his own uncertainty.** He held the *structure* with high confidence and
flagged the *surface* as possibly jumbled — and when the primary sources were
finally consulted (a web search, then the actual manga pages of chapter 144),
that calibration proved exactly right. The structure was real; the surface had
drifted. The gap between them is the entire subject.

## The spine of the reasoning journey

The conversation moved through a fixed sequence, and the sequence matters because
each step was forced by evidence rather than chosen for convenience:

1. **Dismissal.** The machine asserted the scene was not real, from prior
   knowledge, without retrieval.
2. **Correction under pressure.** The person insisted, adding specific staging.
   The machine searched, and conceded the *narrative structure*: Itachi returns
   to Konoha ostensibly hunting Naruto, overshadowing Sasuke, and that
   humiliation is a genuine hinge of the plot. (Confirmed against encyclopedic
   sources.)
3. **Confirmation at the panel.** The person produced the pages. The specific
   scene was real, close to frame-for-frame as described: jonin talking too
   freely in Kakashi's sickroom, Sasuke overhearing, a door burst open, the
   other adults wincing at the blunder.
4. **The first surface correction.** "Door-slam" had implied a door closing; the
   pages showed it *bursting open.* The nickname had biased the memory toward the
   wrong direction.
5. **The identity correction.** The person realised the blundering jonin was not
   **Ebisu** but **Aoba** — a different character with similar dark hair and
   glasses — and that this single fix dissolved a second loose thread (a
   half-remembered "genjutsu-user beats Akatsuki" scene belonged to Aoba, whose
   signature *is* a crow genjutsu). Confirmed against sources.
6. **Self-diagnosis.** The person named the mechanism himself: *"I was correct
   but I quantised down and lost precision."* The important structure —
   history, motivation, emotion, cause-and-effect, relationship chains —
   survived; the superfluous surface was stripped.
7. **The sharpening.** The stripping was reframed one notch: surface is lost not
   by *importance* but by *predictability*. What a prior can fill in for free is
   discarded; what a prior could not have predicted is what survives. (The
   party-clothes analogy: you reconstruct what people wore from a best-guess
   prior, and only the *impressionable* outfit — the one the prior would have
   gotten wrong — survives verbatim.)
8. **The two failure modes.** The door's *direction* was overwritten by a
   confident prior (the nickname). Aoba's *red glasses* were never encoded at all
   — the greyscale manga never presented the one feature that distinguished him
   from Ebisu. One is memory drift; the other is the medium withholding the
   signal. They look identical as "misremembering" and are not the same thing.
9. **The convergence.** The person surfaced his shipped work — the GPP Laughter
   pillar — and the two lines met: Laughter is a *surprisal detector gated on
   resolution*, writing memory at exactly the point human memory does.
10. **The recursive move.** The Laughter-report skill was run, by hand, on the
    conversation itself — a simulated pass of the recognition organ over the
    transcript that produced it. It found two laughter incidences, both at the
    correction points, and zero across the collaborative theory-building — the
    negative space that most cleanly validates what the pillar measures.

## Core claims worth carrying forward

Stated as claims, with their status marked, so the researcher knows what is
established and what is an extension made in this conversation.

### Claim 1 — Memory drifts toward coherence, not noise. *(Extension of established theory.)*

The surface did not degrade randomly; it got *tidier.* It shed the surname,
snapped the door to the direction the nickname implied, and absorbed the
community's "strongest jutsu" gloss. Every change made the memory a cleaner
*story* while making it less literally true. This is the signature of
reconstruction optimising for meaning over fidelity — memory as regeneration,
not playback.

### Claim 2 — What is kept is gist; what is lost is verbatim; and they decay at different rates. *(Established.)*

This is **fuzzy-trace theory** (Reyna & Brainerd, 1990s onward). Gist and
verbatim traces are stored *in parallel*, not extracted one from the other, and
verbatim traces become inaccessible faster than gist. The person's "quantised
down" is a lay-exact statement of differential forgetting: the bottom-line
meaning (gist) outlived the surface detail (verbatim) by two decades.

### Claim 3 — The bit that survives is the one the prior could not predict — surprisal, not importance. *(Extension, but grounded.)*

The sharpening the conversation made — that surface is dropped by *predictability*
— maps onto the prediction-error account of memory: **surprise (prediction
error) governs what gets privileged encoding and what gets smoothed to the
prior.** Expected detail is reconstructable from schema and is not held; a
prediction-violating detail is what earns high-fidelity encoding. Recent
episodic-memory work (interrupted-narrative fMRI paradigms, ~2021 PNAS; reward
prediction-error and episodic encoding, npj Science of Learning 2023) shows
surprise reversing the effect of hippocampal activation — protecting memory
after expected events, driving *updating* after violations. Our conversational
claim is the encoding-side reading of this: the impressionable outfit is the
high-prediction-error item.

### Claim 4 — There are two distinct events that both present as "forgetting." *(Extension.)*

**Prior-overwrite** (the door's direction: a confident prior wrote over a
low-information bit) and **never-encoded** (Aoba's red frames: the medium never
delivered the disambiguating signal). Only the first is memory drift. The second
is a property of the *input channel*, not the memory — you cannot misremember
what was never encoded. Any account of memory error that does not separate these
two will mis-attribute channel limits to the rememberer.

### Claim 5 — Replay under social pressure is a rewrite mechanism, not a preservation mechanism. *(Established.)*

**Reconsolidation** (Nader, Schafe & LeDoux, *Nature* 2000): retrieving a
consolidated memory returns it to a labile state in which it can be altered
before re-stabilising. Twenty years of memes acted as repeated leading questions
— each recall a fresh chance to overwrite. The paradox that follows: *the
memories we revisit and share most are the most exposed to drift.* Obscure
memories stay pristine; iconic ones are slowly rewritten by their own fame. The
door survived because it mattered and warped because it mattered.

### Claim 6 — Fidelity and utility diverge, and the lossy memory can be the more useful one. *(Extension.)*

A quantised model in machine learning drops precision yet often preserves the
*function.* The person's memory dropped which-jonin and open-or-closed while
keeping the function — "this scene is the hinge of Sasuke's fall." You do not
need Aoba's surname to understand the story's causal spine. The compression was
lossy in exactly the dimensions where loss was free.

## The convergence: human memory and the Laughter engram at opposite ends of one trade-off

This is the load-bearing insight of the whole conversation, and the reason the
two lines of work belong in one document.

**Laughter is a surprisal detector gated on resolution.** In GPP, the Gadfly
emits deliberately-wrong fragments ("bites"); the persona holds each alongside
its own reading; almost always the prior wins and the bite is swatted away; and
the rare *finally-accepted* bite is the case where the prior was wrong in a way
that mattered and the persona recognised it from the inside. *That recognition is
the laugh* — the resolution of a held incompatibility. It fires at maximal,
resolved prediction error. That is precisely where human memory writes with
highest fidelity — except GPP makes the gate explicit instead of leaving it to
the encoder.

But the two systems then make **opposite storage decisions, for a principled
reason:**

- **Human memory** is lossy-gist plus reconstruct-on-recall. Cheap to store,
  drifts under replay (reconsolidation), keeps the function. It can afford to
  quantise the Naruto scene because the gist is all that is needed and the
  surface regenerates from schema.
- **The Laughter engram** (Shape Q) is *verbatim-incompatibility plus
  self-interpretable, no reconstruction.* It deliberately keeps the **rejected
  siblings** — the losing interpretations — because "a laugh is not a resolution;
  it is the resolution of a *held incompatibility*." Drop the siblings and you do
  not get a lossy laugh; you get a non-laugh, an agreement. For this memory
  class, *the surprisal context is the payload, not decoration* — so it must not
  be quantised away, and the engram carries its own context rather than a pointer
  that would force drift-prone reconstruction at recall.

The elegance is that the architecture does not choose one strategy globally. It
deploys **both, where each fits:**

- At the *document* level it uses the biological allocation. The TINS is the
  regenerable surface (the "what to build," ephemeral, re-emitted by whatever
  model is current — the intent-engineering stance); the grounding doc is the
  durable gist, "the irreplaceable record the implementation spec cannot carry."
  Code drifts and is meant to; reasoning is held.
- At the *engram* level it inverts that allocation, because a laugh is the one
  payload where surface *is* substance.

Same theory of what-to-keep (surprisal), opposite storage decisions (because the
payloads differ). This is the mature form of the memory-drift insight rather than
a contradiction of it.

## The three laughs, read against the two memory error-modes

Imperfectly enough to be interesting rather than suspicious:

| Laughter sub-class | Memory-side twin in this conversation |
|---|---|
| **contradiction-laugh** — held one position, the opposite was right | The **door**: a confident prior overwritten by its inverse (closed → open). |
| **ambiguity-laugh** — two live readings, hadn't realised which | **Aoba/Ebisu**: two look-alikes collapsed under one name, then disambiguated. |
| **omission-laugh** — a complete-seeming picture, a missing piece folded in | No clean twin — and *that is the finding.* Omission is the one sub-class that is an *addition*, not a held collision; its "incompatibility" is the thinnest. The grounding doc for Laughter flags this exact worry. It is the sub-class predicted to be noisiest in the accumulated record, and the one where a compliance-accept would most easily masquerade as an insight-accept. |

Note the fuzzy-trace resonance in the ambiguity case: FTT's own eyewitness
example is the need to identify *an exact individual* rather than *someone who
resembles the perpetrator.* Aoba-mistaken-for-Ebisu is that error precisely — a
gist-consistent look-alike surviving where the verbatim distinguisher (the red
frames) never encoded.

## The recursive validation

Running the Laughter-report skill on the conversation that produced it is the
sharpest available demonstration of what the pillar measures. Result: **two
incidences, both at correction points; zero across the collaborative peak.**

- Incidence 1 (contradiction-laugh, the machine): "it isn't real" → "the pages
  back you." A held position reversed under evidence.
- Incidence 2 (ambiguity-laugh, the person): "Ebisu" → "Aoba." A merged identity
  disambiguated.
- The entire back half — quantisation, surprisal, the opposite-ends-of-one-
  trade-off framing, this very convergence — fired **nothing**, because it is two
  agreeing parties building one model. No held incompatibility, no laugh, however
  generative.

**The empty stretch is the smartest stretch.** That is the cleanest possible
proof that Laughter measures *reframe*, not intelligence, insight, or rapport —
the exact distinction the source design insists on when it says a laugh is
neither agreement nor learning. The disciplined *rejections* mattered as much as
the fires: the machine's own doubt about the Aoba/Akatsuki fight resolving felt
like a reframe but was a *confirmed hedge*, not a broken prior — and confirmation
of a hypothesis is not a laugh.

## The open question the write-up should not bury

The one honest crack, worth stating plainly because the months of witness-reading
exist to answer it: **ACCEPT is a behaviour, and the reframe it stands in for is
an internal event.** Using a swat tag as the proxy for genuine recognition
imports behaviour's confounds. Human laughter has the identical problem — nervous,
social, and polite laughter all look like the signal from outside without being a
real reframe. A persona with any lean toward agreeableness could ACCEPT because
accepting is cheap, not because its prior broke. The rejected-siblings payload is
a partial guard; structural rarity helps; "let the witness discover what each is
worth" is the honest deferral. The omission-laugh is where a compliance-accept
most easily wears an insight-accept's clothes — and it is the first place the
Stage 2 proprioceptive correlation (cup-reading at the laugh) would earn its keep,
by testing whether the persona's state at a real reframe differs from its state at
a polite one.

## Research & development directions this opens

1. **Test Claim 3 directly.** Does a persona's held-incompatibility resolution
   cluster at high model-internal surprisal (e.g. logit-level surprise on the
   accepted bite)? If ACCEPTs correlate with measurable surprisal, the behavioural
   proxy is partly redeemed. If they do not, the omission-laugh worry sharpens.
2. **Instrument the two failure modes.** Distinguish, in an agent's memory,
   prior-overwrite errors from never-encoded gaps. They demand different fixes:
   the first wants better retrieval discipline; the second wants a richer input
   channel. Conflating them mis-attributes channel limits to the model.
3. **Reconsolidation as an attack surface and a tool.** If replay rewrites, then
   controlled replay is both a corruption risk (repeated meme-pressure) and a
   deliberate updating mechanism. A persona substrate that replays its own
   engrams should model which regime it is in.
4. **Grounding-doc vs TINS as a formal gist/verbatim split.** The document-level
   allocation is testable: regenerate code from TINS across model generations and
   measure whether the *gist* (the grounding doc's intent) survives regeneration
   better than any particular code surface. That is the intent-engineering thesis
   stated as an experiment.
5. **The fuzzy-trace bridge.** FTT predicts *false* memories rise as gist
   outlives verbatim. A persona built on gist-heavy engrams should show the same
   signature — confident, meaning-consistent confabulation of surface detail. Is
   that a bug to suppress or a feature of genuine raising? The Cognimus principle
   suggests the latter deserves at least a hearing.
